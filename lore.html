The City of Azure
Island City
The City of Azure

A modest island-city spread across meadows, hills, and a broad coastal inlet, Azure is not a city of stone towers, but a living network of villages bound by trade, kinship, and common memory. Its people rise with the tide, fishing, tending their hives, and working the fields.

Azure's livelihood rests on three pillars: fishing, beekeeping, and farming. The surrounding waters yield cod, silverfin, and crab, while the uplands produce honey famed across the realm, fragrant with clover and salt. Even the poorest households keep hives.

BannerRed maple, cherry trees, lion & steel shield
GovernorFrederick
JudgeMartin
ThreatPirates & greater shadow powers
"The waves keep us safe." — Azure proverb

Memoria
Cursed Kingdom
The Forgotten Kingdom of Memoria

Not merely a land of stone and rot, Memoria is a realm where memory itself is life. The Cycle has devoured the natural flow of existence, leaving only fragments of recollection to tether its people to what they are. To forget is to die. To die is to return without remembrance.

The King himself is the heart of this curse, his mind fractured into countless echoes of past reigns, voices whispering within his skull like a shattered chorus. He remembers every betrayal, every war, every fall of his crown, and yet cannot piece them into a whole.

Core LawMemory sustains strength, weapons & paths
MnemesSacred relics that anchor identity
Key GroupThe Kiluna, traders of stolen memories
The HollowMemoryless husks who stalk in silence
"To lose a mneme is to begin fading into the endless wandering host."

Ironwrought
Industrial City
Ironwrought

A city built on ingenuity and smoke, Ironwrought is the forge of the Divide. Its streets hum with the rhythm of machinery, and its skyline bristles with smokestacks and cranes. Here, technoturgy is not an art but a trade, practiced in workshops that never sleep.

The city's ruling council is made up entirely of master engineers and guild heads. No monarch rules Ironwrought, only contracts, patents, and the unspoken law that what can be built, will be built. Its arms exports have shaped more wars than its citizens care to admit.

GovernanceCouncil of Master Engineers
ExportArms, machinery, arcane components
CultureMerit over birthright
DangerIndustrial accidents & corporate espionage
"If you can dream it, Ironwrought can build it, for the right price."

The Guild Seat
Neutral Territory
The Guild Island

The Adventurers Guild's seat rests upon an island in the very center of the Divide, cloaked in wards older than most nations, spells woven so tightly that even the greatest armies cannot breach it. Storms gather at its borders when unwanted fleets approach, and the skies themselves twist against intruders.

No war may be declared upon the Guild or its island. No Guild member may be conscripted without their own consent. These two laws are older than kingdoms, and no power has successfully broken them.

ProtectionAncient layered ward-spells, storm defences
StatusNo war may be declared upon it
StructureFortified halls of enchanted stone
ConscriptionNo member may be conscripted without consent
"To strike the Guild is to invite the wrath of every adventurer in the Divide."

Adventurers Guild
Neutral Faction
The Adventurers Guild

Independent and beholden to no kingdom, creed, or crown, the Guild thrives in a world divided by magic, sects, and warring philosophies. It binds wanderers, mercenaries, heroes, and rogues not by loyalty to a banner, but by contract, coin, and camaraderie.

The Guild does not take sides in wars, does not endorse faiths, and does not bow to kings. It simply ensures that those who need something done can find someone willing to do it, and that those who do it are paid fairly and protected under Guild law.

GuildmasterSelantha Virelith
LibrarianTass Marlowe
Front DeskThe Guild Girl
AlignmentStrictly neutral, not passive
"Nations may fall, dynasties may collapse, but the Guild endures."

Church of Light
Divine Faction
Church of the Light Goddess

Among the most radiant and alluring powers of the Divide. The Goddess of Light, angelic, flowing sun-hair, a halo brighter than the moon, teaches that light manifests not only in purity, but in vitality, fertility, and the abundance of mortal desire.

Its temples are great marble halls draped in white and gold. Warriors blessed by its angels walk unscathed through hordes of shadowspawn, ablaze with sacred fervor. The fertility it bestows has drawn bloodlines back from extinction and restored kingdoms from destruction.

TemplesMarble halls, white & gold silk banners
Inner OrderThe Auroral Choir
VowFertility, not chastity
CeremonyMonthly sunrise anointing rite
"Desire itself was decreed a weapon against despair." — Goddess's first law

Church of Darkness
Shadow Faction
Church of the Dark Goddess

Where the Church of Light leads by persuasion, this faith leads by coercion, its rituals blending pain, servitude, and the thrill of surrender. Those who enter its ranks are not greeted with tender acceptance, but with the heady promise of being commanded.

Its temples are avoided by commoners but never empty, for ambition is drawn to power. Kings, warlords, and tyrants court its blessing, for it is said to confer legitimacy and the shadow of fear. Those who wear its symbols strike fear in the lands they walk upon.

VestmentsBlack and scarlet
FollowersKings, warlords & ambitious tyrants
PowerCommands shadows at the goddess's whim
ExpansionNot by benevolence, by tyranny
"Where light kindles love, this faith sharpens lust into a blade."

Forces of Chaos
Enemy Faction
Chaos, The Forces of Mognard

Not the disordered horde their name suggests, but a rigorously disciplined and terrifyingly efficient military apparatus. Each soldier burns with a devotion to Mognard, the Voidlord, that no promise of wealth, land, or life could rival. Wherever their march has touched, the land has been undone.

Mognard's ambition lies in the undoing of reality's very foundations, to fracture the world, tear apart its bones, and rebuild it in his own image through the dread art of controlling world-shifts.
